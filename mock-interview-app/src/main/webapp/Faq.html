Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the "request an interview" tab disappear after I request an interview?

At the moment, we only allow one active (unexpired) interview request or scheduled interview as the interviewee, per person. Therefore, we remove the option to go to the "request an interview" page while you have an active interview request listing or scheduled interview as the interviewee. If you have a scheduled interview as the interviewer, you can still make a request to be interviewed.

I want to give an interview but I don't see a button to accept an interview request on the listings page. How do I accept one?

From the listings page, you can click on a listing that interests you, and a page with the full interview request details will appear. On that page, there will be an easily identifiable button to accept an interview request.

Where will the mock interview take place?

In the interview request listing, you will be able to see urls to the coding environment of the interviewer's choice and a Google Hangouts chat, which is where the coding and communication will take place, respectively.


Please note: Some companies do not allow interviewers to use an IDE (Integrated Development Environment) during the interview, so we suggest that interviewers choose an evironment without a code "auto-complete" feature.

Does the spoken communication for the interview have to be via Google Hangouts?

At the moment, yes, as we want to ensure that communication is done safely and effectively. While several reliable communication platforms exist, the Match Mocker team feels that uniformly requiring use of the Google Hangouts platform will allow for the safest and most secure experience for our users.

During the interview, should I have my camera on?

It is up to you, but we suggest that the camera be shut off, as it will allow for an experience more similar to the technical phone intervews that companies tend to give. Additionally, having both the interviewer and interviewee shut off their cameras will enhance safety and privacy for both parties.

What's the benefit of being the mock interviewer?

There are many benefits to being the mock interviewer, but to name a few:

  • Giving an interview can help you improve your understanding of the type of (algorithm/data structure) question that you give.
  • Experiencing what it's like to be the interviewer will help you become more aware of what an interviewer looks for from an interviewer.
  • You will learn from what the person you are interviewing does and doesn't do well.
